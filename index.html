Open House Prague is an annual festival that gives participants the opportunity to enter buildings in the city that are typically not open to the public. More than 100 buildings are open to the public, including modern and historic buildings, apartments, offices, studios and other venues. The aim of the event is to raise awareness of the city's architectural and artistic values, as well as its cultural diversity, all for free.

Prague is the capital and largest city of the Czech Republic and the 14th largest city in the European Union. Located on the banks of the Vltava River, Prague is home to around 1.3 million people, while the capital's agglomeration has an estimated 2.7 million inhabitants. The city has a temperate oceanic climate with relatively warm summers and cold winters. Prague is one of the political, cultural and economic centres of Central Europe, with a rich history. Founded in Roman times and flourishing in the Gothic, Renaissance and Baroque periods, Prague was the capital of the Kingdom of Bohemia and the main residence of several German-Roman emperors, notably Charles IV (1346-1378). It was an important city for the Habsburg Empire and the Austro-Hungarian Empire. The city played a relevant role in the Czech and Protestant Reformation, the Thirty Years' War, and in 20th century history as the capital of Czechoslovakia during the World Wars and the post-war communist era. Prague is home to many famous cultural attractions, many of which survived the violence and destruction of 20th century Europe. Highlights include Prague Castle, Charles Bridge, Old Town Square with its astronomical clock, the Jewish Quarter, Petřín Hill and Vyšehrad. Prague's extensive historic centre has been a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1992. The city has more than ten museums, as well as numerous theatres, galleries, cinemas and other cultural institutions. An extensive modern public transport system links the city's districts. It is home to a number of public and private schools, including Charles University in Prague, the oldest university in Central Europe. Since 2017, the city has welcomed more than 8.5 million international visitors a year, making it the fifth most visited European city after London, Paris, Rome and Istanbul. In 2019, the PICSA Index ranked it the 13th most liveable city in the world. The name 'Golden Prague' is thought to date back to the reigns of the Czech King Charles I and the German-Roman Emperor (known as Charles IV) (1347-1378), when the towers of Prague Castle were clad in gold. According to another theory, Prague was named 'gold' during the reign of the alchemists, and also during the reign of the German-Roman Emperor Rudolf II, who encouraged goldsmiths.
